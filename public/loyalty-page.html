Loyalty Programs - Susan Maupin Case Study

Challenge

What do you do if your company has many loyal consumers, but the majority of them regularly shopped your competitors? This was the challenge of a $350MM organic yogurt company who needed to change their consumers’ purchase behavior and deepen loyalty.

Solution

To learn more about the consumer and why they purchased other yogurts, I first led an attitude and usage research study. We discovered that although many consumers don’t understand the concept of organic, the benefits of organic are meaningful. We also learned that our consumers rely heavily on “influencers”-i.e. family/ friends/experts-in making purchase decisions.

To deepen the understanding of organic and to leverage the value these consumers placed on “influencers” I launched a two-tier loyalty program.

  • The first tier was a Rewards program aimed at reaching as many of the company’s consumers as possible and incentivizing them to buy the product.
  • The second tier was an ambassador program geared towards the company’s most loyal advocates with the goal of promoting them as influencers and fostering word of mouth in a grassroots way. We created an innovative platform that encouraged our ambassadors to leverage social media to engage with and inspire other consumers.

Results

We engaged and educated the company’s consumers in a fun and inspiring way. We grew the rewards program to 400,000+ members. The website we created generated over 1 million page views per month with a 70% return rate. The ambassador program grew to 8,000 advocates who were regularly influencing and educating more than 1 million connections on Facebook.
