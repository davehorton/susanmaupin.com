Product Launch - Susan Maupin Case Study

Challenge

What would be your approach if your Fortune 100 company decided to launch a new retail concept as a brand extension of its existing stores featuring unique and distinctive service offerings, requiring a paradigm shift on the part of your customers?

Solution

I quickly recognized that everyone in the organization was talking about the company’s new offering very differently. I put together an internal team and developed our positioning and messaging. I hired an advertising agency, provided them with strategic and creative direction, and worked with them to finalize language and create marketing, sales and promotional materials.

I worked closely with other members of the team to design and create an exciting in-store experience—from layout of the store footprint, to signage to messaging throughout the stores.

Additionally, to prep the launch:

  • I met with members of the Chamber of Commerce and key business leaders in each community, gaining their input and participation in our launch event.
  • I developed an "office makeover contest" in which business owners submitted essays as to why they should win an office makeover. At our launch events, we announced winners and gave each a check for their makeover.
  • I developed a joint co-marketing program with Microsoft, Intel, Cisco, HP and Symantec; marshaling resources across all companies to support the launch.

Results

As a result, we held the most successful launch events in this Fortune 100 company’s store history, and created a new template for future launch events. We forged new and strong partnerships with key vendors. And, we created a strong positioning that gave clarity to the organization’s offering and its benefits to the consumer, thus enabling the sales team to improve their effectiveness.
