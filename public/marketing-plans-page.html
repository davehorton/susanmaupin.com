Marketing Plans and Strategy - Susan Maupin Case Study

Challenge

What would you do if a key business unit, which had previously been highly profitable, began to shows signs of decline? I was charged with identifying the challenges and outlining possible solutions for turning around this division of a Fortune 100 office supply company.

Solution

I began by conducting a business assessment, asking critical questions to get at the heart of the problem. I set up test cells and did an analysis to determine the business’s current and future potential for profitability.

After concluding that the business had viable potential, I took a look at the attention the business was receiving throughout the organization. It quickly became apparent that marketing was acting as a silo and not taking full advantage of all of the resources and people in the organization that could help grow the business. I set up cross-functional teams which led to cooperation, changes in compensation plans for the tele-sales team who we incentivized to focus on this business, and improvements in a telesales user interface with enhanced customer intelligence.

I realized that analytics and modeling were being underutilized and created a working partnership with the modeling team. Together we created models that improved customer selection and developed customer segments, which allowed us to optimize our marketing resources.

To address the need for new growth, I introduced the program to a new segment of customers—which led to significant increases in revenue for this business unit as well as contributing to new purchase in other categories, making them stickier customers.

Results

As a result of these efforts and others, we grew the business to $763 MM – a 110% increase over the course of 2 years.
